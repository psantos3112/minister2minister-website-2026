Wellness

Spiritual Pause

By Hal and Pamela Santos · One.64 Leadership

Spiritual wellness is connected to meaning, values, purpose, hope, and the beliefs that guide how a person lives and leads.

This reflection invites leaders and teams to slow down long enough to pay attention to the condition of the inner life. Spiritual wellness is not limited to one setting or one kind of organization. It is about the values and convictions that provide moral guidance, shape decisions, and sustain a sense of purpose.

The simple invitation is to take a spiritual pause — an honest moment to notice where you are, what has shaped you, and what kind of support you may need.

P

Pulse

Take an honest read of your current reality. Notice where you have been, what you have carried, and how recent experiences have affected your heart, mind, and relationships.

A

Awareness

After you recognize what is happening beneath the surface, decide how you will respond. Awareness becomes meaningful when it leads to honesty, healing, and wise next steps.

U

Uniqueness

Remember that your life has purpose. Whether you are new to your role or have served for decades, you matter to your family, team, organization, and community.

S

Secret

Pay attention to what you may be carrying alone. Hidden pain can isolate people, but asking for help is not weakness. It is often the beginning of freedom.

E

Everybody Wins

Your wellness affects more than you. When you care for your spiritual health, your family, work, department, community, and future all benefit.

A spiritual pause is not a luxury for quiet seasons. It is a practical rhythm for people who carry responsibility. The healthier the leader, the healthier the people and places touched by that leader’s life.
